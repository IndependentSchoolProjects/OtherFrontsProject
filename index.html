Timeline

900-720 BCE: The Palestine region is owned by the Jewish Kingdoms of Israel and Judah before being conquered by the Assyrians

33 CE: Christian prophet Jesus is crucified in Jerusalem by the Romans

621 CE: Islamic prophet Muhammad goes to Jerusalem before ascending to Heaven. Around this time, Arabs become the majority population in the Palestine region

1096-1291 CE: Christian Crusaders from Europe under the Pope and Byzantine emperors invade the Middle East and create the Christian Kingdom of Jerusalem in the Palestine region

1516 CE: The Ottoman Empire conquers the Palestine region from the Egyptian Mamluks

1915-1917 CE: The Ottoman Empire joins WW1 on the side of the Central Powers. To undermine their rule in the Middle East, Britain promises to create an independent state to both Arabs and Jews in the region if they fight against the Ottoman Empire

1920 CE: The British are given the Mandate of Palestine to administer until it is able to self-govern

1947 CE: The United Nations General Assembly votes to partition the British Mandate of Palestine into the Arab state of Palestine, located in Gaza and the West Bank, and the Jewish state of Israel, located in Jerusalem

1948 CE to Present: Palestine and Israel have fought multiple wars over the region. Most recently in 2023, Hamas in the Gaza strip has attacked Israel and is experiencing retaliation

How was Palestine Involved in World War 1?

During the First World War, Palestine was under Ottoman rule. However, the British were keenly interested in it and requested it in the Sykes-Picot Agreement of 1916. This territory was greatly useful to the British for two main reasons. Firstly, it would secure the Suez Canal from land invasions, suxh as the Ottoman one at the start of the war. Secondly, Palestine is located in a key region between Europe, Africa, and Asia. British control over the region would guaruntee Britain's overseas holdings, especially India.
